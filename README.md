# Geo Shopping Assistant

**Members:** Marlon Badilla - Daniel Campos - Issac Corrales - Guillermo Coto - Kevin Espinoza - David López

## Context
The sector is grocery retail in Costa Rica, an industry composed of 5 major chains (Walmart/Mas x Menos/Pali, Auto Mercado, MegaSuper,
Gessa, and PriceSmart) operating over 350 formal supermarkets, complemented by approximately 13,000 pulperías and mini-markets. The
supermarket market value in Costa Rica reached ~$300 million USD by 2023, serving a population of 5 million plus ~2 million annual tourists. A
mid-format supermarket handles between 15,000 and 30,000 SKUs (unique products), distributed across an average area of 30,000–40,000
square feet with 10 to 20+ aisles. Each store employs between 30 and 150 workers depending on format.The specific problem to be solved is
the inefficiency in locating products within the store, both for employees answering customer inquiries and for customers themselves. New
employees can take weeks to memorize product locations, customers waste time walking through unnecessary aisles, and restocking and
expiration-control staff lack tools to optimize their work routes. Every shelf reorganization invalidates the team's accumulated knowledge.

## Idea
An intelligent product location platform that centralizes the physical location of every item (aisle, shelf, section, side) in a database. Employees
access it through a handheld device to instantly search for products and optimize restocking and expiration review tasks. Customers access it
through an app or kiosk that, given their shopping list, calculates the optimal route within the store. An admin panel allows updating locations
when the store is reorganized. AI optimizes routes, prioritizes operational tasks, and estimates costs for the customer.
